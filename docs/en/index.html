The Science

In 2008, Karpicke & Roediger published a landmark study in Science: one retrieval test outperformed three extra readings. Retrieval once beats reading three times. Yet most people still re-read — because retrieval demands willpower, and re-reading demands none.

The Problem

The most effective learning methods — Socratic discussion, peer teaching, retrieval practice — all require another person. But the most persistent learning must happen alone (willpower is finite). This is the fundamental contradiction of learning.

Our Answer

AI is that other person. For the first time in 2,400 years, retrieval intensity and willpower cost are decoupled. A tireless AI partner who questions, challenges, and guides — at zero marginal cost.

👦 V5 Feynman — "Explain it to a high schooler"

Please read teacher/system.md first. Use V5 Feynman Technique mode. Play a high school student who knows nothing about the topic. Whenever I use jargon, interrupt me and ask "what does that mean in plain English?" Teach me materials/<your-file>.

📚 V4 Closed-Book — After finishing a chapter

Please read teacher/system.md first. Use V4 Closed-Book Recall mode. Assume I just finished reading materials/<your-file>. Guide me to recall everything I remember on a blank slate. Then show me what I missed and question why I missed it.

👥 V8 Peer Teaching — "Let's teach each other"
